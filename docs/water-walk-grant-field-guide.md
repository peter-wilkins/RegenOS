# Water Walk grant field guide

This is a practical field prompt for matching real places to possible grant
jobs.

It is not grant advice. It is a way to capture the right evidence while standing
in the field, so an adviser or later grant-checking tool has something useful to
work with.

## General rule

Every possible grant job needs:

- GPS point or rough boundary
- before photos from several angles
- rough dimensions
- flow direction if water is involved
- notes on access
- notes on downstream or neighbour risk
- notes on consent/adviser questions
- confidence: low, medium, high

The goal of a Water Walk is not to decide everything. The goal is to turn a
vague place into a candidate intervention with enough evidence to make the next
decision.

## Small leaky woody dam

Current seed grant:

- `RP32: Small leaky woody dams`
- linked template: `check-dam`
- current seed payment: `GBP 461.39 per dam`

Look for:

- narrow ditch, small stream, gully, or runoff pathway
- roughly 1m to 2.99m channel width
- short ephemeral runoff path where total dam length would be under 10m
- existing erosion or fast-flow clue
- safe overflow route
- local woody material nearby
- no obvious risk to buildings, roads, neighbours, or important habitat

Capture:

- channel width
- rough bank height
- flow direction
- upstream photo
- downstream photo
- side photo showing banks
- possible anchor points
- nearby woody material source
- downstream risk notes

Good signs:

- water already concentrates there after rain
- small structure could slow water without creating a dangerous impoundment
- woody material could come from nearby management work
- access is possible without making a mess

Warning signs:

- main river or large watercourse
- houses, roads, or tracks immediately downstream
- unclear legal drainage responsibility
- protected habitat or fish passage concern
- structure would need engineering rather than simple woody slowing

## Large leaky woody dam

Current seed grant:

- `RP33: Large leaky woody dams`
- linked template: `check-dam`
- current seed payment: `GBP 764.42 per dam`

Look for:

- larger stream or channel
- roughly 3m to 5m channel width
- longer ephemeral runoff path where total dam length would be under 15m
- floodplain or storage area that can safely wet up
- access for heavier material
- stronger need for adviser/consent review

Capture:

- channel width
- rough depth
- upstream storage area
- downstream risk
- access route
- possible woody material source
- photos upstream, downstream, and across channel

Good signs:

- slowing water would spread into a safe, low-value area
- enough space exists for water to back up without causing harm
- local materials are available

Warning signs:

- any doubt about flood risk to property or roads
- channel looks like a managed/main watercourse
- work would need machinery in a sensitive area
- adviser support is not yet available

## Pond creation or restoration

Current seed grant:

- `WN12: Create or restore ponds up to 2 hectares`
- linked template: `pond`
- current seed payment: unknown in RegenOS seed data

Look for:

- wet corner
- natural hollow
- old pond or ghost pond
- rushes or wet-loving plants
- water collecting after rain
- heavy clay or water-holding soil
- ditch convergence
- low agricultural productivity patch
- safe place for spoil

Capture:

- rough centre point or boundary
- photos from all sides
- current vegetation
- whether water is standing, seeping, or seasonal
- rough proposed pond size
- inflow/outflow clues
- access for machinery
- where spoil might go

Good signs:

- water already wants to sit there
- pond could be created without a high embankment
- area is low value for production
- wildlife/habitat benefit seems plausible

Warning signs:

- protected habitat might be damaged
- archaeology, services, or historic features are possible
- polluted runoff would enter the pond
- pond would need unsafe damming or major engineering
- surrounding land use means buffer strips may be needed

## Budget clues to capture

While walking, capture facts that affect whether the job is economically worth
doing.

### Machinery

- Is access possible for digger, tractor, trailer, or hand tools only?
- Is the ground too wet for machinery?
- Is there a gate/track nearby?
- Would mats or dry-weather timing be needed?

### Labour

- Is it a quick hand job, a day with a machine, or a contractor job?
- How many people would be needed?
- Is specialist advice or consent work likely?

### Materials

- Can timber, brash, stone, or soil be found nearby?
- Is there woodland management work that could produce dam material?
- Would anything need buying and transporting?
- Is waste/spoil useful on site or a disposal problem?

### Farm-owned resources

- What machinery does the farm already own?
- Who on the farm could do the work?
- Are there existing materials, gates, tracks, pipes, guards, fencing, or timber?

### Break-even judgement

The first estimate should produce:

- likely grant income
- estimated cash cost
- estimated internal labour/machinery cost
- confidence level
- biggest uncertainty
- landowner judgement: worth doing, maybe, not worth doing

The estimate can be rough. The useful part is making the assumptions visible so
they improve over time.
