# Grant lifecycle task guide

This is the short "do it right" guide for generated grant lifecycle tasks.

It is not grant advice. It is a practical checklist for JobDone tasks so the
landowner, adviser, and agent know what evidence to gather and what mistakes to
avoid.

## Explore

### Review candidate and rough budget

- Stand at the site if possible.
- Capture photos in at least four directions.
- Record GPS location or boundary.
- Write the plain-English reason the site is interesting.
- Record rough size: length, area, width, number of structures, or tree count.
- Make a first budget: grant income, cash cost, internal time, machinery, and unknowns.
- Decide whether it is worth exploring, needs adviser input, or is probably not worth it.

Good evidence:

- clear before photos
- map pin or boundary
- rough dimensions
- note of likely grant option
- list of unknowns

Common mistakes:

- starting with the grant code instead of the site problem
- ignoring access, spoil, maintenance, or downstream risk
- pretending a rough estimate is precise

## Pre-Application

### Check Rural Payments land and maps

- Confirm the business and land parcels are registered.
- Check the field parcel matches where the work will happen.
- Check boundaries, ineligible features, and digital maps.
- Save screenshots or references if they help the later application pack.

Good evidence:

- parcel references
- map screenshot or exported boundary
- note of any mismatch to fix

### Check management control and existing funding

- Confirm who has management control of the land.
- Check tenancy, licence, landlord, or common-land constraints.
- Check whether the same work, land, or outcome is already funded.
- Note any double-funding risk.

Good evidence:

- management-control note
- existing agreement references
- landlord or tenant approval where needed

### Check consents and protected constraints

- Check Environment Agency, lead local flood authority, internal drainage board, local planning authority, Natural England, Forestry Commission, highways, HEFER, SSSI, archaeology, utilities, and public access as relevant.
- Record "not needed" evidence as well as granted permissions.
- Put unresolved consent questions in the unknowns list.

Good evidence:

- consent documents
- emails or adviser notes
- map overlays
- "not required" confirmation where available

Common mistakes:

- assuming small water work needs no consent
- forgetting archaeology or historic environment constraints
- forgetting downstream neighbours

### Collect before-work evidence

- Take dated photos before any work starts.
- Include wide context and close-up detail.
- Photograph upstream/downstream for water work.
- Photograph access and spoil/material locations.
- Record dimensions and GPS.

Good evidence:

- before photos
- GPS point, line, or area
- dimensions
- access notes
- constraint notes

## Application

### Prepare and submit application pack

- Use the official scheme route.
- Include the right grant option, parcel, quantity, and evidence.
- Attach adviser support, quotes, maps, plans, or specifications where required.
- Save the submitted version and reference number.

Good evidence:

- application copy
- reference number
- submission date
- attached documents index

Common mistakes:

- submitting without required adviser support
- missing quotes for actual-cost items
- using the wrong unit or quantity

## Agreement

### Record agreement offer and dates

- Record offer date, acceptance date, agreement start date, end date, claim deadline, and maintenance/durability period.
- Compare approved items with the original plan.
- Record anything rejected or changed.

Good evidence:

- agreement document
- dates
- approved option IDs and quantities
- changes from application

## Delivery

### Confirm work is allowed before starting

- Do not start work or buy materials until the agreement is accepted and the agreement start date has arrived.
- Check consents are in place.
- Check the work still matches the approved specification.

Good evidence:

- agreement start date
- consent checklist
- "work allowed" note

### Brief workers or contractors

- Share the approved specification.
- Explain photo, measurement, invoice, and change-record needs.
- Make sure they know what not to disturb.
- Decide who captures evidence during work.

Good evidence:

- contractor brief
- agreed plan/specification
- evidence responsibilities

### Collect delivery records

- Take photos during work where useful.
- Record changes from the plan.
- Keep invoices, receipts, timesheets, and material records.
- Record actual dimensions and quantities.

Good evidence:

- during-work photos
- invoice or labour record
- materials record
- actual dimensions
- change notes

Common mistakes:

- losing invoices
- not recording why the design changed
- only taking photos after everything is hidden or covered

## Claim

### Assemble claim pack

- Gather after photos, invoices, receipts, timesheets, maps, dimensions, and permissions.
- Check every evidence requirement for the grant option.
- Make an index of evidence files.
- Mark missing items clearly.

Good evidence:

- claim checklist
- evidence index
- after photos
- cost records
- consent records

### Submit claim through official route

- Submit only through the official route.
- Save claim date, claim reference, and submitted evidence list.
- Record any follow-up questions from the funder.

Good evidence:

- claim reference
- submitted claim copy
- submitted evidence index
- follow-up messages

## Payment Review

### Review estimate against actuals

- Record actual grant paid.
- Record actual cash cost and internal labour/machinery cost.
- Compare with the estimate.
- Explain why it differed.
- Record what went better, what went worse, and what to assume next time.

Good evidence:

- payment record
- final cost summary
- variance explanation
- lessons learnt

Common mistakes:

- treating the job as finished when payment arrives
- not learning from cost overruns or easy wins

## Maintenance

### Schedule durability and maintenance checks

- Record maintenance obligations and end date.
- Create inspection reminders.
- Take repeat photos from the same locations.
- Record repairs, failures, blocked structures, tree losses, grazing issues, or invasive species.

Good evidence:

- maintenance schedule
- repeat photos
- inspection notes
- repair records
- survival or condition checks

Failures to capture:

- structure bypassed by erosion
- tree guards failed or trees died
- pond polluted or dried too quickly
- swale silted up
- grassland seed failed because fertility or management was wrong
- downstream or neighbour issue appeared

These are not embarrassing. They are how the next job gets better.
