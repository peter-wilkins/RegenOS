# Vision

RegenOS is a land restoration operating system.

It should help people move from vague good intentions to funded, practical, evidenced work on real land.

## The problem

A farmer or landowner may have land that could support ponds, check dams, tree planting, wetland restoration, hedgerows, agroforestry, or natural flood management. But the path from idea to delivery is messy:

- government schemes are confusing
- eligibility rules change
- evidence requirements are easy to miss
- maps and environmental constraints are scattered
- contractors are hard to coordinate
- invoices and photos are often gathered too late
- long-term maintenance obligations are forgotten

Most existing systems focus on either mapping, farm management, grant advice, or compliance. RegenOS should connect the whole chain.

## The opportunity

The durable problem is not "apply for this year's scheme". Governments change schemes constantly.

The durable problem is:

> Plan, fund, execute, evidence, claim, maintain, and monitor regenerative land work.

That problem exists across countries.

## The system should support

- farmers and landowners exploring what is possible
- advisers designing projects
- contractors delivering work
- funders understanding what was delivered
- volunteers completing field tasks
- software agents assembling evidence packs
- researchers improving suitability models

## Long-term ambition

A user should be able to select a farm or catchment and ask:

- Where should we hold water?
- Where should we plant trees?
- Where should we reconnect floodplain?
- What should be fenced?
- Which interventions stack well together?
- Which funding sources are relevant?
- What tasks need doing this month?
- What evidence is missing?
- What did we actually achieve?

## What success looks like

A restoration project should leave behind a complete digital trail:

- map
- design rationale
- funding source
- task list
- responsible people
- photos
- GPS points
- measurements
- invoices
- before/after evidence
- inspection reports
- maintenance schedule
- ecological and hydrological outcomes where possible

That trail should be useful for grant claims, private funding, biodiversity markets, water company catchment schemes, and long-term learning.
